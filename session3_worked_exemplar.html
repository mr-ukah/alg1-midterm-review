Q2
Linear Systems · Intersection · Evaluating Statements
Given f(x) = (2/3)x − 4 and g(x) = (1/4)x + 1, four statements are given. Which are true?
(a) II, only (b) IV, only (c) I and IV (d) II and III ✓
Plan of Attack
Strategy: find the intersection first, then check each statement

If you know where the two lines cross, you can check all four statements quickly. Don't check each statement by itself without knowing the intersection — you'll spend too much time.

Reading the statements carefully

Statement I says "f(4) = g(4)" — this means "at x = 4, both functions give the same output." Statement III says "the graphs intersect at (12, 4)" — this means x = 12 AND y = 4 simultaneously.

⚠️ Don't confuse "when x = 12, f(x) = g(x)" with "they intersect at (12, something)." Statement II says x = 12 is the x-coordinate of the intersection. Statement III checks both coordinates. They say the same thing two different ways.
↗ Study Guide: Linear Systems
Full Solution
1Find the intersection: set f(x) = g(x)
(2/3)x − 4 = (1/4)x + 1 Multiply every term by 12 (LCM of 3 and 4): 8x − 48 = 3x + 12 5x = 60 → x = 12 Find y: f(12) = (2/3)(12) − 4 = 8 − 4 = 4 Intersection = (12, 4)
2Check each statement
I. f(4) = g(4)? f(4)=(2/3)(4)−4=−4/3. g(4)=(1/4)(4)+1=2. −4/3 ≠ 2. FALSE ✗ II. At x=12, f(12)=g(12)=4. TRUE ✓ III. Intersection at (12,4). TRUE ✓ IV. Intersection at (4,12)? No — intersection is (12,4), not (4,12). FALSE ✗
✓
Answer (d) II and III Intersection is (12, 4). Statements II and III both correctly describe this point. I and IV are false.
Q9
Factoring · Cubic with Leading Negative
When factored completely, −x³ + 10x² + 24x is
(a) −x(x + 4)(x − 6) (b) −x(x − 4)(x − 6) (c) −x(x + 2)(x − 12) ✓ (d) −x(x − 2)(x + 12)
Plan of Attack
Always factor out the GCF first

Every term has x. The leading coefficient is negative, so factor out −x (not just x). This flips the signs of everything inside — be careful.

After factoring out −x: factor the trinomial

You'll have a trinomial x² + bx + c. Find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b.

⚠️ Sign flip trap: When you factor out −x, every sign inside reverses. The original +10x² becomes −10x inside, and +24x becomes −24 inside. Write it out slowly before proceeding.
↗ Study Guide: Factoring
Full Solution
1Factor out −x
Every term has x; leading term is negative → factor out −x: −x³ + 10x² + 24x = −x(x² − 10x − 24) Check: −x · x² = −x³ ✓ −x · (−10x) = +10x² ✓ −x · (−24) = +24x ✓
2Factor the trinomial x² − 10x − 24
Find p, q: p · q = −24, p + q = −10 Try +2 and −12: product = −24 ✓, sum = −10 ✓ x² − 10x − 24 = (x + 2)(x − 12)
✓Combine and verify
−x(x + 2)(x − 12) Verify: −x · (x+2)(x−12) = −x(x²−10x−24) = −x³+10x²+24x ✓
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(a) −x(x+4)(x−6): (x+4)(x−6) = x²−2x−24. After −x: −x³+2x²+24x ≠ original (b) −x(x−4)(x−6): (x−4)(x−6) = x²−10x+24. After −x: −x³+10x²−24x ≠ original (sign of last term wrong) (d) −x(x−2)(x+12): (x−2)(x+12) = x²+10x−24. After −x: −x³−10x²+24x ≠ original (sign of middle term wrong)
✓
Answer (c) −x(x + 2)(x − 12) Factor out −x (flips signs), then factor x²−10x−24 using +2 and −12.
Q12
Transformations · Absolute Value · Horizontal Shift
Which function represents the graph of h(x) = |x| shifted 3 units to the right?
(a) g(x) = |x + 3| (b) p(x) = |x − 3| ✓ (c) r(x) = |x| + 3 (d) w(x) = |x| − 3
Plan of Attack
Horizontal shift rule: changes go inside the function

To shift a graph horizontally, you replace x with (x − h) inside the function. A shift RIGHT by 3 means replacing x with (x − 3). This feels backwards, but here's why it works: the vertex of |x| is at x = 0. After the shift, you want the vertex at x = 3. |x − 3| = 0 when x = 3. That's correct.

⚠️ The direction is opposite the sign inside. |x − 3| shifts RIGHT (not left). |x + 3| shifts LEFT (not right). Adding outside the function shifts vertically — |x| + 3 goes UP, |x| − 3 goes DOWN.
↗ Study Guide: Transformations
Full Solution
1Apply the horizontal shift rule
Shift RIGHT 3 → replace x with (x − 3) inside the function h(x) = |x| → p(x) = |x − 3| Vertex check: |x−3| = 0 when x = 3. Vertex at (3, 0). ✓ Shifted right 3.
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(a) |x+3|: vertex at x = −3. That's a shift LEFT 3, not right. (c) |x|+3: adds outside → shifts UP 3. Vertex moves to (0, 3). That's vertical, not horizontal. (d) |x|−3: subtracts outside → shifts DOWN 3. Vertex moves to (0, −3). Also vertical.
✓
Answer (b) p(x) = |x − 3| Shift right 3: replace x with (x−3) inside. Vertex moves from (0,0) to (3,0).
Q4
Quadratic Models · Projectile Motion · Quadratic Formula
h(t) = −16t² + 5t + 105. How many seconds to fall 45 feet below the starting point?
(a) 1.45 (b) 1.84 ✓ (c) 2.10 (d) 2.72
Plan of Attack
Read carefully: "45 feet below starting point" ≠ height of 45

The diver starts at 105 feet. Falling 45 feet below that starting point means reaching a height of 105 − 45 = 60 feet. Set h(t) = 60 (not 45).

Then use the quadratic formula

Once you set h(t) = 60, rearrange to standard form and apply the quadratic formula. Take the positive root — time can't be negative.

⚠️ The most common error on this problem: setting h(t) = 45. That gives a different (wrong) equation. Always translate "below starting point" into an actual height first.
↗ Study Guide: Quadratic Models
Full Solution
1Translate the question into an equation
Starting height = 105. Fall 45 feet below → new height = 105 − 45 = 60. h(t) = 60 −16t² + 5t + 105 = 60 −16t² + 5t + 45 = 0 16t² − 5t − 45 = 0
2Apply the quadratic formula
a = 16, b = −5, c = −45 t = (5 ± √(25 + 4·16·45)) / (2·16) t = (5 ± √(25 + 2880)) / 32 t = (5 ± √2905) / 32 t = (5 ± 53.90) / 32 Take positive root: t = (5 + 53.90)/32 = 58.90/32 ≈ 1.84 sec
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(a) 1.45: comes from setting h(t) = 45 instead of 60. Wrong height target. (c) 2.10: comes from a rounding or setup error mid-calculation. (d) 2.72: comes from taking the wrong (negative) root and adjusting, or from a different incorrect equation.
✓
Answer (b) 1.84 seconds Set h(t) = 60 (not 45). Solve 16t²−5t−45=0 with the quadratic formula. Take the positive root.

Q16a
Linear Functions · Context · Writing from a Table
Colleen makes friendship bracelets. The table shows cost f(x) for x bracelets.
Write a linear function f(x). Explain what the slope and y-intercept mean in context.
Plan of Attack
Step 1: Find slope from two table rows

Pick any two (x, f(x)) pairs. Slope = (change in f(x)) ÷ (change in x). Then check with another pair — if all pairs give the same slope, it's linear.

Step 2: Find b (y-intercept)

Use y = mx + b with any known point. Plug in the slope and the (x, y) values and solve for b.

Step 3: Interpret in context

Slope = rate of change per bracelet. Y-intercept = starting cost (when 0 bracelets are made). Both need to be stated in the language of the problem — dollars and bracelets, not just numbers.

↗ Study Guide: Linear Models
Full Solution
1Find slope using two rows
Using (4, 7.50) and (6, 9.00): m = (9.00 − 7.50) / (6 − 4) = 1.50 / 2 = 0.75 Verify with (6, 9.00) and (9, 11.25): m = (11.25 − 9.00) / (9 − 6) = 2.25 / 3 = 0.75 ✓ consistent
2Find the y-intercept
Use y = 0.75x + b with point (4, 7.50): 7.50 = 0.75(4) + b 7.50 = 3.00 + b → b = 4.50
3Write the function and interpret
f(x) = 0.75x + 4.50
Slope = 0.75: The cost increases by $0.75 for each additional bracelet made. This is the per-bracelet material cost.
Y-intercept = 4.50: When 0 bracelets are made, the cost is $4.50. This is a fixed starting cost — the cost of basic supplies needed before making any bracelets.
✓
Full Credit (4 pts) f(x) = 0.75x + 4.50 1 pt: correct slope 0.75 with work. 1 pt: correct y-intercept 4.50 with work. 1 pt: slope interpreted in context ($0.75 per bracelet). 1 pt: y-intercept interpreted in context ($4.50 fixed cost).

Q18
Systems of Equations · Write · Graph · Interpret
Ski team: 5 members in 2010, +10/year. Chorus: 35 members in 2010, +5/year. Let x = years since 2010.
(a) Write a system. (b) Graph on the axes provided. (c) Explain the intersection.
Plan of Attack
Part (a): translate story → y = mx + b

The starting amount (in 2010) is the y-intercept b — it's what you have when x = 0. The yearly increase is the slope m. Write one equation per group.

Part (b): graph both lines

Plot the y-intercept of each line, then use the slope to find one more point. Draw the line and label it. Do this for both equations. Mark the intersection point clearly.

Part (c): interpret BOTH coordinates

Don't just say "they're equal." Say WHEN they're equal (x-value → year) AND how many members each group has (y-value) at that moment.

⚠️ Label your lines on the graph. Without labels, the grader can't tell which line is the ski team and which is the chorus. You'll lose a point for unlabeled graphs.
↗ Study Guide: Systems & Graphing
Full Solution
1Part (a): Write the system
Ski team: starts at 5 (b=5), grows by 10/year (m=10) y = 10x + 5 Chorus: starts at 35 (b=35), grows by 5/year (m=5) y = 5x + 35
2Part (b): Find the intersection (algebraically)
10x + 5 = 5x + 35 5x = 30 → x = 6 y = 10(6) + 5 = 65 Intersection = (6, 65)
For graph: plot y-intercepts (0,5) and (0,35), then intersection (6,65). Label each line.
3Part (c): Interpret both coordinates
x = 6 means 6 years after 2010, which is the year 2016. y = 65 means both the ski team and the chorus each have 65 members. Full interpretation: In the year 2016, both the ski team and the chorus had 65 members. After this point, the ski team (growing faster at 10/year) will be larger than the chorus.
✓
Full Credit (6 pts) System: y=10x+5 and y=5x+35. Intersection: (6, 65). 1 pt: ski team equation. 1 pt: chorus equation. 1 pt: graph both lines correctly. 1 pt: intersection marked at (6,65). 1 pt: x=6 interpreted (6 years / 2016). 1 pt: y=65 interpreted (65 members each).
