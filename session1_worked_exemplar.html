Q6
Linear Equations · Slope from Non-Standard Form
What is the slope of the line whose equation is 4x = 3(y + 8)?
(a) 4/3 ✓ (b) 3/4 (c) −4/3 (d) −3/4
Plan of Attack
Strategy: rearrange into y = mx + b form

Slope is only readable once y is isolated on one side. The coefficient of x after that isolation is the slope. Distribute, move terms, divide.

⚠️ Do not flip the fraction. After rearranging, the coefficient of x is 4/3 — not 3/4. The 3 divides both sides, making slope 4/3, not the ratio of the original coefficients in reverse.
↗ Study Guide: Linear Models
Full Solution
1Distribute the right side
4x = 3(y + 8) 4x = 3y + 24
2Isolate y — divide everything by 3
3y = 4x − 24 y = (4/3)x − 8 Slope = 4/3
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(b) 3/4: flipped the fraction — the coefficient of x is 4/3, not 3/4. (c) −4/3: negative sign error. Dividing by 3 gives +4/3, not −4/3. The slope is positive — this line rises left to right. (d) −3/4: both errors combined — flipped the fraction AND added a wrong negative sign.
✓
Answer (a) 4/3 Rearrange to y = (4/3)x − 8. The slope is the coefficient of x after y is isolated: 4/3.
Q3
Consecutive Integers · Algebraic Representation
Which expression represents the product of two consecutive odd integers, where n is an odd integer?
(a) n(n + 1) (b) n(n + 2) ✓ (c) n(n + 3) (d) 2n + 1
Plan of Attack
Key fact: consecutive odd integers differ by 2, not 1

Odd integers skip the even integers between them. The odd integers are ..., 3, 5, 7, 9, ... Each one is 2 more than the previous. So if the first odd integer is n, the next one is n + 2.

Product means multiply

The product of two numbers means you multiply them together: n × (n + 2) = n(n + 2).

⚠️ n(n+1) is two consecutive integers, not two consecutive odd integers. If n = 5, then n+1 = 6, which is even — not the next odd number. You need n+2 = 7 to get the next odd.
↗ Study Guide: Consecutive Integers
Full Solution
1Find the next consecutive odd integer after n
Odd integers: ..., 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, ... Gap between consecutive odds = 2 If first odd = n, next odd = n + 2
2Write the product and check with a number
Product = n(n + 2) Check: if n = 5, next odd = 7. Product = 5 × 7 = 35. n(n+2) = 5(7) = 35 ✓
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(a) n(n+1): product of consecutive integers (any), not consecutive odds. If n=5: 5×6=30, but the next odd is 7, not 6. (c) n(n+3): skips too far. If n=5: 5×8=40. But the next odd after 5 is 7, not 8. (d) 2n+1: this is just a single expression for an odd number (not a product of two numbers). Doesn't represent multiplication of two integers.
✓
Answer (b) n(n + 2) Consecutive odd integers differ by 2. If first is n, next is n+2. Product = n(n+2).
Q10
Functions · Vertical Line Test
The graph below shows curves of best fit for four relations A, B, C, D comparing temperature to altitude. Which relation is NOT a function?
(a) A (b) B ✓ (c) C (d) D
Plan of Attack
Strategy: apply the vertical line test to each curve

A relation is a function if every x-value (here: temperature) gives exactly one y-value (here: altitude). On a graph, drag an imaginary vertical line across. If it ever hits the curve in two places at once, that relation is NOT a function.

Look for the curve that doubles back horizontally

Curves A, C, and D all go in one direction — up or down — without curving back on themselves. Curve B curves back to the left, meaning at some temperatures there are two different altitudes. That's the failure.

⚠️ A curve can curve — it just can't curve back horizontally. A curve going up then down (like a parabola opening sideways) will fail the vertical line test. A curve going up then up more steeply is still a function.
↗ Study Guide: Functions & Domain
Full Solution
1Apply the vertical line test to each curve
A: moves steadily — one altitude per temperature. FUNCTION ✓ B: curves back on itself. At certain temperatures, the curve reaches two different altitudes. A vertical line hits it in two places. NOT A FUNCTION ✗ C: moves steadily — one altitude per temperature. FUNCTION ✓ D: moves steadily — one altitude per temperature. FUNCTION ✓
✓
Answer (b) B Curve B doubles back horizontally — at some x-values (temperatures) it gives two y-values (altitudes). It fails the vertical line test.
Q5
Arithmetic Sequences · Common Difference
What is the common difference in the sequence 2a + 1, 4a + 4, 6a + 7, 8a + 10, ...?
(a) 2a + 3 ✓ (b) −2a − 3 (c) 2a + 5 (d) −2a + 5
Plan of Attack
Common difference = subtract any term from the term after it

In an arithmetic sequence, you get from one term to the next by adding the same amount every time. That amount is the common difference. Compute (term 2) − (term 1) and verify with (term 3) − (term 2).

⚠️ Subtract in order: later term minus earlier term. If you do it backwards you get the negative of the common difference. Also subtract the entire expression — both the a-term and the constant.
↗ Study Guide: Sequences
Full Solution
1Subtract term 1 from term 2
(4a + 4) − (2a + 1) = 4a + 4 − 2a − 1 = 2a + 3
2Verify with term 3 minus term 2
(6a + 7) − (4a + 4) = 6a + 7 − 4a − 4 = 2a + 3 ✓ consistent
✗Why the wrong choices are wrong
(b) −2a−3: subtracted in the wrong order — did (term 1)−(term 2) instead of (term 2)−(term 1). The negative of the right answer. (c) 2a+5: subtracted only the constant parts and ignored the a-terms, or used the wrong pair of terms. (d) −2a+5: mixed errors — wrong sign on a-coefficient and wrong constant.
✓
Answer (a) 2a + 3 (4a+4) − (2a+1) = 2a+3. Verified: (6a+7) − (4a+4) = 2a+3. ✓

Q14
Quadratic Equations · Discriminant · Rational vs. Irrational Roots
Is the solution to 0 = 2x² + 3x − 10 rational or irrational? Justify your answer.
Plan of Attack
Use the discriminant: b² − 4ac

The discriminant is the expression under the square root in the quadratic formula. It tells you what kind of solutions you get without making you solve the whole equation. If it's a perfect square (0, 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ...) the roots are rational. If not, the roots are irrational.

What rational and irrational mean here

Rational roots can be written as exact fractions (like 2, −1/2, 3/4). Irrational roots involve a square root that doesn't simplify to a whole number (like √89 — which you can't simplify further).

⚠️ You must show the calculation and state the conclusion. Just writing "irrational" with no work earns only 1 out of 2 points. Show the discriminant calculation, then explain why that value leads to irrational roots.
↗ Study Guide: Quadratic Models
Full Solution
1Identify a, b, c
2x² + 3x − 10 = 0 a = 2, b = 3, c = −10
2Calculate the discriminant
b² − 4ac = (3)² − 4(2)(−10) = 9 − (−80) = 9 + 80 = 89
3Interpret the result
Is 89 a perfect square? Check: 9² = 81, 10² = 100. 89 is between them — NOT a perfect square. √89 cannot be simplified to a whole number or a fraction. Conclusion: The solutions are IRRATIONAL.
✓
Full Credit Answer Irrational — discriminant = 89, which is not a perfect square 1 pt: correct discriminant calculation (89). 1 pt: correct conclusion stated with reasoning (89 is not a perfect square → irrational roots).

Q16b
Exponential Models · Depreciation · Writing and Evaluating
A car was purchased for $12,000. It depreciates at an average rate of 14.5% per year.
(a) Write V(t), the car's value t years after purchase.
(b) Find how much the car depreciates from year 3 to year 4, to the nearest cent.
Plan of Attack
Exponential decay model: V(t) = a · b^t

The starting value a = $12,000 (what you paid at t = 0). The base b is the fraction of value the car keeps each year. If it loses 14.5% per year, it keeps 100% − 14.5% = 85.5% each year. So b = 0.855.

Part (b): depreciation = V(3) − V(4)

Depreciation from year 3 to year 4 means how much the value dropped during that one year. Compute V(3) and V(4) separately, then subtract. Keep full decimal precision until the final subtraction.

⚠️ b = 0.855, not 0.145. 0.145 is the depreciation rate — what is lost. The base b is what remains: 1 − 0.145 = 0.855. Using b = 0.145 would mean the car keeps only 14.5% of its value each year, which is far too fast.
↗ Study Guide: Exponential Models
Full Solution
1Part (a): Write the function
Starting value: a = 12,000 Rate kept each year: 1 − 0.145 = 0.855 V(t) = 12,000(0.855)^t
2Part (b): Compute V(3) and V(4)
V(3) = 12,000 × (0.855)³ = 12,000 × 0.625026375 ≈ $7,500.32
V(4) = 12,000 × (0.855)⁴ = 12,000 × 0.534397551 ≈ $6,412.77
3Find depreciation: V(3) − V(4)
$7,500.32 − $6,412.77 = $1,087.55 The car depreciates by $1,087.55 from year 3 to year 4. (Use a calculator. Round only at the final step.)
✓
Full Credit (4 pts) V(t) = 12,000(0.855)^t · Depreciation ≈ $1,087.55 1 pt: correct base 0.855 identified. 1 pt: correct function written. 1 pt: correct V(3) and V(4) computed. 1 pt: correct depreciation stated to the nearest cent.
